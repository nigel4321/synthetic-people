Spike 2 result — 2026-05-09
Verdict: PASS on all four checks (parent-RSS bound, mmap-share,
write throughput, read throughput). Phase 5d's Arrow-IPC zero-copy
mmap pattern is viable on this host; green-light Phase 5d.1
implementation.

Notable:
  - System RAM delta during the 8-worker mmap-read phase was
    +0.01 GB. Sum of per-worker reported RSS was 7.83 GB. The
    apparent-to-physical ratio of ~700:1 confirms kernel page-cache
    sharing works essentially perfectly for our access pattern.
  - The per-site Python loop (`for i in range(my_slice.shape[0]):
    alt_count = int((my_slice[i] > 0).sum())`) — the realistic
    5d.1 worker pattern, identical in shape to what killed Phase
    5e Phase A through refcount-COW divergence — ran cleanly on
    all 8 workers in 0.69 s with zero physical RAM growth. Numpy
    mmap views avoid the refcount-COW problem because numpy holds
    one PyObject wrapper for the whole array, not one per element.
  - Data-correctness sanity: each worker counted alt alleles in
    1,250 samples × 100,000 sites at 5 % allele frequency →
    expected ~6,250,000. All 8 landed in 6,246k–6,257k. Random
    sampling distribution looks right.
